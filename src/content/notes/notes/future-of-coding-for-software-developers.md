## Coding as a standalone skill is being commoditized

Boris Cherny (head of Claude Code) hasn't edited a line by hand since November 2024; Karpathy hasn't since December 2024 [[head-of-claude-code-what-happens-after-coding-is-solved]] [[the-end-of-coding-andrej-karpathy-on-agents-autoresearch-and-the-loopy-era-of-ai]]. The bottleneck has shifted from typing code to deciding what to build and why. Martin Fowler frames this as the biggest shift since assembly to high-level languages, but the real discontinuity is moving from deterministic to non-deterministic systems [[how-ai-will-change-software-engineering]].

## The developer role evolves from "engineer" to "builder"

Boris predicts the title "software engineer" fades, replaced by "builder" — someone who blends product sense, design sense, and business acumen [[head-of-claude-code-what-happens-after-coding-is-solved]]. The future belongs to high-agency generalists who identify problems rather than wait for tickets [[the-age-of-the-generalist]]. Lee Robinson's advice: become a generalist, because code is cheap now and taste matters more [[ai-codes-better-than-me-now-what]].

## The industry expands, but individuals may not

Grady Booch shows that every abstraction shift in software history triggered existential dread, then expanded the industry [[the-third-golden-age-of-software-engineering-thanks-to-ai-with-grady-booch]]. Karpathy invokes Jevons paradox: cheaper software creation unlocks latent demand. However, Geoffrey Huntley warns that non-adopters face natural attrition as AI-using peers achieve 16x productivity gains [[some-software-devs-are-ngmi]]. Steve Yegge predicts roughly 50% of engineers at big companies will be cut, with the remainder funded by AI token budgets [[from-ides-to-ai-agents-with-steve-yegge]].

## Agent orchestration is the new skill stack

Karpathy's new productivity metric is tokens commanded per second, not lines typed [[the-end-of-coding-andrej-karpathy-on-agents-autoresearch-and-the-loopy-era-of-ai]]. Steve Yegge maps 8 levels of AI adoption and claims 70% of engineers are stuck at levels 1-2 [[from-ides-to-ai-agents-with-steve-yegge]]. Huntley argues that understanding tool calls, inferencing loops, and agent primitives is now interview-baseline knowledge [[fundamental-skills-and-knowledge-you-must-have-in-2026-for-swe]].

## Systems thinking and architectural judgment are the moat

Booch is emphatic: AI automates known patterns (CRUD apps, standard UI), but defense systems, distributed real-time architectures, and ethical trade-offs remain human problems [[the-third-golden-age-of-software-engineering-thanks-to-ai-with-grady-booch]]. AI lacks architectural judgment — the value shifts from writing code to knowing what code to write and why [[the-age-of-the-generalist]].

## The timeline tension

Dario Amodei sees AGI in 1-3 years and half of entry-level white-collar jobs gone in 1-5 years [[the-day-after-agi]] [[dario-amodei-we-are-near-the-end-of-the-exponential]]. Booch calls this "utter BS" — conflating coding with engineering [[the-third-golden-age-of-software-engineering-thanks-to-ai-with-grady-booch]]. The truth likely lives in Dario's own nuance: capability races ahead, but economic diffusion lags, and the gap is where all the strategy lives.

## Working effectively in the transition

New methodologies are emerging to bridge the gap. Vibe-Driven Development provides structured patterns for AI collaboration through radical transparency, verbose communication, and human-in-the-loop controls [[vibe-driven-development]]. The shift isn't about abandoning rigor — it's about redirecting it from code production to code orchestration and review.

## Gaps

- Junior developer onboarding paths when seniors don't write code
- Non-Western perspectives on the transition
