## Key Takeaways

- Your brain was designed to think, not store. Every time you force it to remember something, you're paying a cognitive tax that shows up in forgotten details, repeated mistakes, and background anxiety.
- Traditional second brains fail because they require "taxonomy work at capture time"—deciding where things go when you're walking into a meeting or about to sleep.
- The shift is from AI as a search tool to AI running a loop: capture → classify → route → surface → nudge. The system works while you sleep.
- You can build this with Slack (capture), Notion (storage), Zapier (automation), and Claude/GPT (intelligence)—no code required.

## The Eight Building Blocks

1. **The Dropbox** — One frictionless capture point. Zero decisions.
2. **The Sorter** — AI classifies into buckets (people, projects, ideas, admin) without you thinking.
3. **The Form** — Consistent schema for each type makes automation possible.
4. **The Filing Cabinet** — Notion databases as the writable, queryable source of truth.
5. **The Receipt** — Audit trail showing what came in and where it went. Trust comes from visibility.
6. **The Bouncer** — Confidence filter prevents garbage from polluting storage.
7. **The Tap on the Shoulder** — Daily/weekly digests that push relevant info to you.
8. **The Fix Button** — One-step correction mechanism ("fix: this should be people, not projects").

## Engineering Principles for Non-Engineers

1. Reduce the human's job to one reliable behavior
2. Separate memory from compute from interface
3. Treat prompts like APIs, not creative writing
4. Build trust mechanisms, not just capabilities
5. Default to safe behavior when uncertain
6. Make output small, frequent, and actionable
7. Use "next action" as the unit of execution
8. Prefer routing over organizing
9. Keep categories and fields painfully small
10. Design for restart, not perfection
11. Build one workflow, then attach modules
12. Optimize for maintainability over cleverness
