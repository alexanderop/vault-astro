## Overview

Based on Andy Weir's 2021 novel, directed by Phil Lord and Christopher Miller, starring Ryan Gosling as Ryland Grace. A middle-school science teacher wakes up on a spaceship with no memory, two dead crewmates, and the small task of saving all life on Earth from an alien microorganism called Astrophage that's dimming the Sun.

## Key Themes

- **Science as survival** — Ryland doesn't fight his way out. He thinks his way out. Every crisis gets solved with the scientific method: observe, hypothesize, test, iterate. The movie makes problem-solving feel genuinely thrilling.

- **Communication across the impossible** — The Rocky relationship is the heart of the film. Two species with zero shared language, biology, or sensory experience building trust and understanding from scratch. It's first contact done right — not with weapons or diplomacy, but with music, math, and shared curiosity.

- **Sacrifice and purpose** — The tension between self-preservation and duty runs through the whole film. Ryland was chosen precisely because he's expendable enough to send, but the movie asks: what happens when the expendable person turns out to be the one who matters most?

## Why It Matters

This is the best sci-fi film in years. Weir's trademark "science the shit out of it" approach translates perfectly to screen — Lord and Miller bring enough warmth and humor to keep 2.5 hours moving without ever dumbing down the science. Ryan Gosling plays the everyman scientist with the right mix of confusion, determination, and genuine wonder.

The Rocky storyline alone justifies a 10/10. An alien made of rocks who communicates through musical chords, animated with puppetry and practical effects — and somehow delivers the most emotionally resonant performance in the film. "I am happy. I am sad" hits harder than any CGI spectacle.

The film also nails something rare: it treats intelligence and curiosity as heroic qualities. No chosen-one destiny, no superpowers — just a guy who paid attention in science class and refuses to give up.
